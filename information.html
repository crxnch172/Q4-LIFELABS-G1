Development of a Typhoon Disaster Preparedness for a Typhoon Government and Student Initiatives
The typhoon formed in an area of low pressure at low latitudes in the west Pacific Ocean near Pohnpei in the Caroline Islands group in the Federated States of Micronesia.
Then after hours, the storm had become a Category 5 Typhoon. And with the dates between November 3-11, Typhoon Haiyan/Yolanda made landfall in the Philippines until
at the end of November 11 when it was announced that Typhoon Yolanda was now out of the Philippine Area of Responsibility (PAR).
Typhoon Yolanda/Haiyan sustained a 10-minute wind speed of 230 km/h and a 1-minute wind speed of 315 km/h while moving at a speed of 40 km/h N.W.
This typhoon alone affected an estimated amount of 16 million people, costing over 6,352 confirmed lives across the Philippines. The damage of Typhoon Yolanda/Haiyan cost the Philippines $2.98 Billion, being the costliest in Philippine history.
